General Maintenance

When you first adopt an animal, the learning curve can be a bit steep. To make sure that your new family member fits in right from the start, we have compiled a list of general resources to help out.

First of all, some basic information about the care and keeping of your new pet is in order: For cats: kittenrescue.org's Kitten Care Handbook provides instructions to care for cats from birth to adulthood.

For dogs: PETA also has good general care guidelines for dogs, some obvious, some not.

Spay and Neuter

In order to keep animals off the streets by preventing unwanted pregnancies, it is important to spay and neuter your pets. This proceedure, explained by the AVMA veteranary organization, represents a large portion of what we do at the SPCA, and moves us closer to achieving a euthanasia-free world.

Training Your Dog

If you adopted a puppy or just want to teach an old dog new tricks, training makes life a lot easier! Training provides your dog with a behavioural framework that will keep them out of trouble, as well as giving you bonding time with your pet. About.com provides a great overview of how to tackle first-time training.
